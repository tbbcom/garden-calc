FAQ About The Garden Calculator

How much soil do I need for a 4x8 raised bed?

For a 4ft Ã— 8ft raised bed at 12 inches deep, you need 10.67 cubic feet or 0.395 cubic yards (approximately 6 bags of 2 cu ft soil). At 18 inches deep, you need 16 cubic feet or 0.593 cubic yards (8 bags). Always add 10% extra for settling.


What's the ideal mulch depth for garden beds?

Apply 2-4 inches of mulch. Fine mulch (shredded leaves, cocoa hulls) works at 2 inches. Coarse mulch (wood chips, bark nuggets) performs best at 3-4 inches. Thinner applications allow weeds; thicker layers prevent oxygen and water penetration.


Should I use square or triangular plant spacing?

Square spacing is easier to maintain with straight rows for weeding and harvesting. Triangular spacing uses space 15% more efficiently and works best for groundcovers, ornamental beds, and mass plantings. For vegetable gardens with frequent maintenance, use square or rectangular spacing.


How many gallons is "one inch of water" for my garden?

One inch of water equals 0.623 gallons per square foot. A 100 sq ft garden needs 62.3 gallons for one inch of watering. Most gardens need 1-2 inches weekly (62-124 gallons per 100 sq ft), depending on plant type, soil, and weather.


How often should I water vegetables?

Water deeply 2-3 times weekly rather than shallow daily watering. This encourages deeper root growth. Sandy soils need more frequent watering (every 2-3 days), while clay soils retain moisture longer (every 5-7 days). Adjust based on rainfall and temperature.


What's the difference between topsoil, garden soil, and potting mix?

Topsoil is raw earth for grading and filling. Garden soil is amended topsoil with compost for in-ground beds. Potting mix is soilless (peat/coco coir + perlite) for containers. Use topsoil for raised beds 12"+ deep, garden soil for in-ground beds, potting mix for pots only.

How do I convert cubic yards to bags?

One cubic yard = 27 cubic feet. For 2 cu ft bags: divide cubic feet by 2. For 3 cu ft bags: divide by 3. Example: 0.5 cubic yards = 13.5 cubic feet = 7 bags (2 cu ft) or 5 bags (3 cu ft). Always round up.


When should I apply compost to my garden?

Apply compost in early spring before planting and in fall after harvest. For new beds, work 2-3 inches into the top 6-8 inches of soil. For established gardens, top-dress with Â½-1 inch twice yearly. Avoid applying near plant stems.


What do the NPK numbers on fertilizer mean?

NPK shows percentage by weight of Nitrogen (leaf growth), Phosphorus (roots/flowers), and Potassium (overall health). 10-10-10 is balanced. Leafy greens need high N (20-10-10). Flowering plants need high P (10-30-10). Root vegetables need high K (10-10-20).
